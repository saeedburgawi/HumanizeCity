# Methodology

## Overview

HumanizeCity follows a research-driven and evidence-based methodology for developing open-source tools that support urban analytics and human-centered city planning.

The project integrates architectural knowledge, urban planning principles, spatial analysis, data science, and artificial intelligence into a reproducible workflow.

---
## From Research to Practice

A significant amount of valuable research in architecture and urban planning remains confined to academic publications, making it difficult for practitioners and decision-makers to apply its findings in real-world contexts.

HumanizeCity seeks to bridge this gap by transforming research outputs into practical, reusable, and open-source digital tools.

Rather than stopping at the publication of research findings, the project aims to convert methodologies, indicators, design standards, analytical frameworks, and decision-support models into software components that can be shared, improved, and applied by researchers, practitioners, public agencies, and the broader open-source community.

This philosophy guides the development of every module within HumanizeCity.

---

## Core Principles

### Evidence-Based Research

Development decisions are informed by scientific literature, empirical data, and measurable urban indicators.

### Open Science

Whenever possible, methodologies, datasets, and source code are openly shared to support transparency and reproducibility.

### Human-Centered Design

Analytical tools are designed to improve the quality of life of people rather than optimize technical metrics alone.

### Reproducibility

Research workflows should be reproducible across different cities, datasets, and research teams.

### Modularity

Each component is developed as an independent module that can be reused or extended.

---

## Development Workflow

HumanizeCity follows an iterative workflow:

1. Define the urban problem.
2. Review relevant literature and planning frameworks.
3. Identify data requirements.
4. Collect or integrate datasets.
5. Process and clean data.
6. Develop analytical models.
7. Validate outputs.
8. Visualize results.
9. Publish documentation.
10. Improve through community feedback.
